Developed a versatile Flutter comic book reader app that enables users to seamlessly read and manage digital comics in multiple formats, including CBZ, CBR (zip/rar), EPUB, and PDF. The app offers a rich and customizable reading experience with the following features:

‐ Comics Management: Lets users organize and access their comic collection from device storage efficiently.
This app combines modern UI elements, customizable themes, and robust file handling to provide an enjoyable and accessible comic reading experience on any device.

Process and Fault Diagnosis with Artificial Neural Network in a Batch Reactor

Bachelor Thesis, B. M. S. College of Engineering | May 2022 – Aug 2023

Project Overview
Developed a robust, two-stage algorithm for process and fault diagnosis in a batch reactor environment, leveraging artificial neural networks and advanced feature extraction techniques. The project focused on enhancing the reliability and safety of chemical processes by enabling early detection and classification of faults.

Key Contributions
Algorithm Development:
- Designed and implemented a two-stage diagnostic algorithm using Python, tailored for batch reactor process data.
Feature Extraction:
- Utilized Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA) to extract the most informative features from high-dimensional process data, improving the model’s ability to distinguish between normal and faulty states.
Data Preprocessing:
- Applied z-score standardization to normalize batch reactor data, ensuring consistent input scaling and improving neural network training stability.
Neural Network Modeling:
- Built and trained an artificial neural network (ANN) for fault detection and classification, optimizing its architecture and hyperparameters for high accuracy.
Performance Achievements:
- Achieved an optimized model with a mean square error (MSE) of 0.0054, indicating highly accurate fault diagnosis and minimal prediction error.

Impact
This project demonstrates expertise in process control, data-driven fault diagnosis, and the application of machine learning to real-world chemical engineering problems. The developed solution enhances operational safety and efficiency by enabling rapid, automated detection of process anomalies in batch reactors.

Design of Heat Exchanger Used in Carbon Capture Plant

Course Project, B. M. S. College of Engineering | Nov 2021 – Mar 2022

As part of a three-member team, I contributed to the design and fabrication of a Shell and Tube Heat Exchanger tailored for a carbon capture plant. The project focused on optimizing heat transfer efficiency and reducing energy consumption, leveraging both advanced simulation tools and manual engineering calculations.

Project Highlights:
Process Design:
‡ Selected a shell and tube configuration for its robustness and suitability in handling the high-pressure, high-temperature conditions typical of carbon capture processes.
‡ Defined process requirements based on the thermal duties and operational parameters of the carbon capture plant.
Sustainable Engineering:
‡ Utilized UNISIM process simulation software to perform dimensional analysis, evaluate heat transfer coefficients, and optimize the exchanger’s geometry.
‡ Conducted manual design calculations in Microsoft Excel to cross-verify simulation results, including sizing, pressure drop, and thermal performance.
Performance Outcomes:
‡ Oversaw the fabrication of the heat exchanger, ensuring adherence to design specifications and safety standards.
‡ Implemented design improvements that resulted in a 15% increase in heat transfer efficiency and a significant reduction in overall energy consumption.
Skills Applied:
† Design Management
† Process Simulation (UNISIM)
† Engineering Calculations (Excel)
† Team Collaboration and Project Documentation

Outcomes
‐ Achieved a substantial boost in process efficiency, directly supporting the plant’s sustainability and operational goals.
‐ Enhanced understanding of the integration between simulation tools and manual engineering methods in real-world equipment design.
‐ Developed practical skills in heat exchanger design, process optimization, and project management within a multidisciplinary engineering context.

This project demonstrates my ability to apply engineering principles, simulation software, and collaborative problem-solving to deliver tangible improvements in process equipment for sustainable industrial applications.

Simulation of Methoxide and Integrated Methanol and Carbon-Capture Plant

Course Project, B. M. S. College of Engineering | Jun 2021 – Sep 2021

As part of a four-member team, I designed and simulated a three-stage chemical plant for the sustainable production of methoxide, integrating methanol synthesis and carbon capture processes. Utilizing UNISIM process simulation software, we implemented cross-stage conversion of methanol, carbon dioxide, and hydrogen to maximize methoxide yield while adhering to cleaner production principles.

Key highlights of the project include:
Process Simulation: Modeled the entire plant workflow in UNISIM, optimizing reaction conditions and equipment sizing for each stage to ensure efficient conversion and minimal resource consumption.
Sustainable Engineering: Incorporated advanced carbon-capture techniques and zero liquid discharge strategies, aligning the process with green chemistry and sustainable manufacturing practices.
Performance Outcomes: Achieved an 83% methoxide yield, demonstrating the viability of integrating methanol synthesis with carbon capture for environmentally responsible chemical production.
Collaboration: Worked closely with team members to troubleshoot simulation challenges, analyze process data, and present findings, strengthening both technical and teamwork skills.

This project showcased my abilities in process simulation, sustainable plant design, and the application of advanced engineering tools to real-world chemical engineering challenges.
